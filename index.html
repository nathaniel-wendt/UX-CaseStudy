Case Study :  Unity Notes

My role:   Branding, Visual Design, UX/UI, Prototyping

Inspiration is everywhere but being able to capture and share your ideas with a team is cumbersome with existing software.  All the note taking web apps I have used in the past were either too basic (which didn't allow me to collaborate effectively) or too complicated (which made them frustrating to use). Unity Notes is a prototype web application I developed which gives teams an easy and intuitive way to collaborate online - ensuring great ideas are never lost.

About Me

32 year old Colorado transplant of 4 years (Born and raised in Iowa). I’m the youngest of five children and definitely a momma’s boy. My interest in design began in 8th grade when I discovered MS Paint could pass as a makeshift CAD program to create plans for the woodworking projects I wanted to make. My first foray into Web Development came a few years later when MySpace went big-time. I learned just enough HTML at the time to make a sweet (read: terrible) profile and since then relied on WYSIWYG editors to do the dirty work. It wasn’t until March of 2017 when I found Bloc's Full-Time Designer Track program and decided to quit my job of 4 years to pursue a career in UX Design.

Five months later and I've learned enough to be dangerous. Bloc's online program is a good starting point but it lacks the real-world opportunity to work in a team setting. My goal is to find an established company who's willing to invest in developing me into a full-fledged Designer (and eventually a Project Manager). In the meantime I will continue to learn all that I can through books, webinars, meetups, and by seeking out local non-profit work. My journey into UX Design has been a whirlwind but I truly enjoy learning all that I can about human behavior and how that translates into designing functional and appealing user experiences.
